BBQ Korean Short Ribs ♥

#1

More Info Google Maps Feedback

4 SERVINGS
US METRIC
Ingredients
  1. 2 shallots (Asian, finely diced)
  2. 1 nashi pear (peeled, cored, grated)
  3. 12 cloves garlic (finely grated)
  4. 3 inches ginger (piece, peeled, finely grated)
  5. 220 mL light soy sauce
  6. 3 Tbsp. sesame oil
  7. 3 Tbsp. gochujang (optional)
  8. 1/4 cup brown sugar
  9. 2 Tbsp. extra virgin olive oil (Cobram Estate Light)
  10. 2 kilograms beef short ribs (cut thinly across the bone**)
  11. 3 spring onions (finely chopped)
  12. 2 liters chicken stock (Massel Liquid)
Directions

In a large pot over a stove, heat stock. Add in all ingredients except for ribs and oil and allow to simmer for 2-3 minutes. Turn heat down and place beef ribs into pot. Allow to braise for 2-3 hours. Take ribs out of pot and put into large oven tray. Take some of the braising liquid and pour into a pan over a hot stove and add oil. Reduce the liquid to a saucy consistency. Pour sauce over beef ribs in oven tray. Put into a 220 degree fan-forced oven for 5-10 minutes until outside is sticky. Serve whole or cut in between the bones for bite-sized pieces. Garnish with spring onion and serve with kim chi and rice if desired. **This can be done by the butcher. The cut is also known as ‘flanken’ and is cut across the bone on the chuck end of the short rib. The slices should be approximately ½ inch thick.

Fiesta Chicken

#2

More Info Google Maps Feedback

4 SERVINGS
US METRIC
Ingredients
  1. 2 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil
  2. 1 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts cut into bite-size pieces
  3. 1/2 medium yellow onion or red onion, chopped (about 1 cup)
  4. 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  5. 2 teaspoons onion powder
  6. 1/2 teaspoons paprika
  7. 1 1/2 teaspoons dried dillweed
  8. 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  9. 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  10. 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper use 1/4 teaspoon or less if sensitive to spice
  11. 1 medium red bell pepper cored and chopped
  12. 1 medium green bell pepper cored and chopped
  13. 1 can reduced-sodium black beans (15 ounces) rinsed and drained
  14. 1 can fire-roasted diced tomatoes in their juices (15 ounces)
  15. 1 1/2 cups instant brown rice* do not use white minute rice or regular rice as these will cookdifferently (see note)
  16. 1 cup low-sodium chicken stock
  17. 3/4 cup freshly grated cheddar cheese
  18. Optional for serving: chopped fresh cilantro, diced avocado, plain nonfat Greek yogurt (or sour cream)
Directions

Sauté the chicken, onion, herbs, and spices in a skillet until they start to brown. Add the peppers, black beans, tomatoes, rice, and chicken stock. Bring to a boil, cover, and simmer on low heat until cooked through. Let stand, covered, for 5 minutes. Top with cheese and additional toppings of your choice. ENJOY!

Trout Tacos ♥

#3

More Info Google Maps Feedback

4 SERVINGS
US METRIC
Ingredients
  1. 16 white corn tortillas
  2. 1/2 pound purple cabbage, thinly sliced
  3. 2 green onions, sliced, green and white parts separated
  4. 1/2 pound coleslaw mix
  5. 3 tablespoons Mexican crema, plus more for serving
  6. 1 teaspoon lime zest, or more to taste
  7. lime wedges as needed, divided
  8. 2 tablespoons cilantro leaves, or to taste
  9. salt and freshly ground black pepper
  10. 4 trout filets
  11. 3 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon tapioca starch
  12. 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  13. 2 teaspoons chipotle powder
  14. 1 teaspoon Mexican oregano
  15. 3 tablespoons vegetable oil for frying, or as needed
  16. 1/2 cup prepared guacamole, or as needed for serving
Directions

Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Wrap tortillas in foil. Bake tortillas in the preheated oven until warm, 8 to 10 minutes. Meanwhile, place cabbage, white parts of green onion, coleslaw mix, 3 tablespoons Mexican crema, lime zest, juice of 2 lime wedges, and cilantro in a large bowl. Toss to coat; season with salt and pepper. Set aside.. Carefully remove skin from trout. Cut each trout filet into 2- to 3-inch pieces. Stir tapioca starch, cumin, chipotle powder, and Mexican oregano together in a shallow bowl. Dip fish pieces into seasoning mixture to coat. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until hot. Add coated fish pieces; cook until golden brown and fish flakes easily with a fork, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer to a drip tray, and season with salt. Using 2 corn tortillas for each taco, divide fish, slaw, and guacamole between the tacos. Garnish each with green tops of scallions. Serve with remaining lime wedges and crema on the side. Enjoy!

Epic Summer Salad

#4

More Info Google Maps Feedback

4 SERVINGS
US METRIC
Ingredients
  1. 400g black beans, drained
  2. 2 large handfuls baby spinach leaves, roughly chopped
  3. 500g heritage tomatoes, chopped into large chunks
  4. ½ cucumber, halved lengthways, seeds scooped out and sliced on an angle
  5. 1 mango, peeled and chopped into chunks
  6. 1 large red onion, halved and finely sliced
  7. 6-8 radishes, sliced
  8. 2 avocados, peeled and sliced
  9. 100g feta, crumbled
  10. handful of herbs (reserved from the dressing)
  11. For the dressing
  12. large bunch mint
  13. small bunch coriander
  14. small bunch basil
  15. 1 fat green chilli, deseeded and chopped
  16. 1 small garlic clove
  17. 100ml extra virgin olive oil or rapeseed oil
  18. 2 limes, zested and juiced
  19. 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
  20. 2 tsp honey
Directions

STEP 1

Make the dressing by blending all of the ingredients in a food processor (or very finely chop them), saving a few herb leaves for the salad. You can make the dressing up to 24 hrs before serving.

STEP 2

Scatter the beans and spinach over a large platter. Arrange the tomatoes, cucumber, mango, onion and radishes on top and gently toss together with your hands. Top the salad with the avocados, feta and herbs, and serve the dressing on the side.
